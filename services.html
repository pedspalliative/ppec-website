What we offer

Education, Consultation, and Mentorship for Your Team

Every engagement starts with a conversation. We build programs around your goals, your team, and your realities.

How we teach

Modular, flexible, and built for your context

We design dynamic learning experiences that are interactive, customizable, and grounded in real-world clinical practice. Programs incorporate short didactic content, small-group discussion, reflection, and skills-based learning, including scripted and unscripted role-play, real-world scenarios, and guided feedback, because the work providers face at the bedside is not abstract, and the training shouldn't be either.

Formats

Multi-day workshops Half- or full-day trainings Longitudinal series Content-expert cohort trainings Retreats On-site intensives Grand rounds Visiting lectures Pre-conference workshops Live & pre-recorded webinars

Available in-person, virtual, or hybrid.

Core offerings

Three ways we work alongside your team

Education & Training

Skill-building grounded in real clinical practice, from communication frameworks to provider well-being.

For: nurses, NPs, physicians, social workers, chaplains, psychologists, child life specialists, creative arts therapists, and any provider caring for seriously ill children.

Request Training →

Topics we cover

  • Fundamentals of pediatric palliative care
  • Communication skills
  • Pain and symptom management
  • Goals of care, advance care planning, and shared decision-making
  • Navigating healthcare transitions
  • Interdisciplinary team dynamics
  • Developmental understanding of illness, death, and dying
  • Grief and bereavement
  • Ethical challenges
  • Culture, religion, and spirituality
  • Considerations for adolescents and young adults
  • Compassion fatigue, moral distress, and provider well-being

Consultation

Guidance for complex cases, team dynamics, and program design.

For: hospital administrators, medical directors, nursing leadership, department heads.

Schedule a Consultation Call →

What we deliver

  • Clinical consultation for complex cases or team dynamics
  • Program development and evaluation for new or expanding pediatric palliative care services
  • Leadership and team development for emerging programs

PPEC provides education and program guidance, not medical advice for individual patient management. In accordance with HIPAA, specific patients are never discussed.

Mentorship

Formal mentorship, professional growth and development, and resilience-building support, because skill alone isn't enough.

For: clinicians at any stage, whether new to palliative care, experienced and facing burnout, or part of a team in transition.
